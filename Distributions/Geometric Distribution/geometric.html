Geometric Distribution Visualization

The geometric distribution models the number of trials required to achieve the first success in a sequence of independent Bernoulli trials. The probability of achieving the first success on the \( k \)-th trial is given by the formula:

\( P(X = k) = (1 - p)^{k - 1} \cdot p \)

In this formula, \( (1 - p)^{k - 1} \) represents the probability of failing \( k - 1 \) times, and \( p \) is the probability of success on the \( k \)-th trial.
