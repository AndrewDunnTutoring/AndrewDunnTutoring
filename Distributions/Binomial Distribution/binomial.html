Binomial Distribution Visualization

The binomial distribution models the number of successes in a fixed number of independent trials, each with the same probability of success. The probability of exactly \( k \) successes in \( n \) trials is given by the formula:

\( P(X = k) = \binom{n}{k} \cdot p^k \cdot (1 - p)^{n - k} \)

In this formula, \( \binom{n}{k} \) (read as "n choose k") represents the number of ways to choose \( k \) successes out of \( n \) trials. The term \( p^k \) denotes the probability of achieving exactly \( k \) successes, and \( (1 - p)^{n - k} \) accounts for the probability of the remaining \( n - k \) trials resulting in failures.

When the number of trials (n) is large, the binomial distribution can be approximated by a normal distribution. This is due to the Central Limit Theorem, which states that the distribution of the sample mean approaches a normal distribution as the sample size increases.
